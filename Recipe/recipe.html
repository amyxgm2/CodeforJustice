Peach Protein x 5 462 kcals | 20g P | 80g C | 6g F Breakfast: Makes 5 Ingredients: 500g natural yoghurt 500g vanilla protein yoghurt 3 tins peaches 300g tropical muesli 1. Drain your peaches and optional you can rinse with cold water. Using scissors, cut them into smaller pieces. 2. In a large mixing bowl, combine your 2 yoghurts and 4 tbsps of peaches. Optionally you can add a tsp of vanilla extract at this point. Mix until fully combined. 3. Take your containers and add 1/5 of your remaining peaches into each. 4. Then lay over 1/5 of your yoghurt mix. 5. Finally, layer over 60g of your tropical muesli 6. Pop a lid on and put in the fridge overnight or until ready to eat. 7. Serve and enjoy! Storage: Keep in the fridge until ready to eat. These will last a maximum of 4 days. That means you’ll have to set aside the muesli and peaches for the 5th day and have them with something other than the yoghurt. I haven’t in the video for social media purposes. 1 Tray Chicken Fajitas 441 kcals | 41g P | 44g C | 12g F Ingredients (makes 6) 6 tortilla wraps 900g Chicken Thighs, sliced 2 onions, sliced 2 peppers, sliced 35g fajita seasoning 100g grated mozzarella Optional: 3 tsp lazy garlic + 3 tsp lazy chilli Method: 1. To a large oven proof dish, add your onions, peppers and chicken thighs. 2. Season with your salt, pepper, paprika, optional lazy garlic and chilli and fajita seasoning packet. Mix until fully coated. 3. Cover and then oven bake at 190 degrees for 40 minutes. Remove from the oven and drain the juices. 4. Top with 100g grated mozzarella and then put back in the oven, uncovered, for 5-10 minutes or until the cheese is golden brown. 5. Remove from the oven and mix until the cheese is incorporated. 6. When you are ready to eat, add 1/6 of your fajita mix to a wrap (with some optional side salad). Wrap up. 7. Serve and enjoy! Storage and re-heating: The chicken mix can be stored in the fridge for up to 4 days or in the freezer for up to 2 months. I would recommend portioning out and storing the mix rather than making all the wraps and then storing in the tortillas. When you are ready to eat, microwave the chicken mix until piping hot and then follow step 6. To reheat from frozen allow for more time and stir intermittently. 1 Dish Spag Bolognese 551 kcals | 47g P | 57g C | 13g F Ingredients (makes 6) 750g Beef Steak Mince 5% 300g spaghetti pasta 1 carrot, diced 1 onions, diced 1 pepper, diced 300g low fat cottage cheese 150g Grated Mozzarella 2 jars of bolognese sauce 360ml hot water Optional: 4 tsp lazy garlic + 4 tsp lazy chilli Method: 1. Add 166g gram of bolognese sauce to an oven proof dish. 2. Then add 50g broken spaghetti, 1/6 of your carrot, pepper and onion, 50g cottage cheese and season with Italian herbs and paprika. 3. Break up 125g of beef mince ever the top and then season with salt and pepper. 4. Add 60ml hot water to each dish and cover with tin foil. 5. Repeat steps 1-4 until you have 6 dishes prepped. 6. Bake in the oven for 30 minutes at 190 degrees. 7. Remove from the oven, mix well and then top each dish with 25g grated mozzarella. Put back into the oven for 5-10 minutes or until the cheese has melted. 8. Allow dishes to cool. 9. Serve and enjoy! Storage and re-heating: These meals can be stored in the fridge for up to 4 days or in the freezer for up to 2 months. To reheat from chilled, I microwave them with the lid partially on for 3 minutes. To reheat from frozen allow for more time and stir intermittently.
